    • Dog Care!

    Proper dog care includes a balanced diet, regular exercise, and veterinary check-ups.

    Diet

    To make sure your dog is as healthy and intelligent as possible, make sure you give them a blend of vitamin-packed carbohydrates, fats, and proteins.

    • High-quality dog food contains essential nutrients that keep your pet's coat shiny, their immune system strong, and their muscles healthy
    • Puppies require more protein and fat, while adult dogs need a balanced diet to maintain energy levels
    • Avoid feeding your dog toxic foods like chocolate, grapes, onions, and excessive salt

     

    Source: American Kennel Club

    Grooming

    The level of grooming needed varies from dog to dog. Some dogs have short, smooth fur while others have long fluffy hair. It is important to learn your dog's type of hair so you can make sure that they are properly groomed.

    • Short hair should be brushed at least once a week
    • Long hair should be brushed daily to remove shed fur and keep hair tidy
    • Double coat: Use a special de-shedding tool once a week to get both layers of fur

     

    Source: Humane World

    Training

    Training is an essential part of dog ownership. A well-trained dog is happier, safer, and easier to live with. Teaching commands will help you create a stronger bond with your dog and make your dog much more obedient.

    • Basic Commands: Teach "sit," "stay," "come," and "leave it" for better control
    • House Training: Establish a consistent schedule for potty breaks and reinforce good behavior
    • Leash Training: Help your dog walk calmly on a leash to prevent pulling

     

    Source: Americain Kennel Club
